About Us

Welcome to iHEALTH (Integrative Health) Lab!
We are a young and dynamic research group dedicated to integrative healthcare through cutting-edge research, global collaboration, and education. Our team embraces complementary therapies including acupuncture, herbal medicine, and exercise to promote health and manage complex diseases. We are led by Dr. Mingxiao Yang, MD (CMD), PhD, a physician-scientist trained at Harvard Medical School and Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center, now Assistant Professor at NTU Singapore.

Join Us: We welcome undergraduate/graduate students, postdocs, and collaborators. Email mingxiao.yang@ntu.edu.sg to connect.

Lab Focus

Our research focuses on:

  • Integrative Therapies: Using acupuncture, herbal medicine, Tai Chi, and exercise for cancer-related side effects, metabolic syndrome, and cardiovascular disease.
  • Mechanistic Research: Neurobiological, behavioral, and omics approaches to study cognition, sensory function, and memory.
  • Healthcare Innovation: Enhancing access to therapies via digital tools, AI systems, and implementation science.

Projects

Ongoing Projects:

  • Tai Chi for Chemotherapy-Induced Peripheral Neuropathy: A randomized waitlist-controlled trial funded by the Harvard Osher Center.
  • Massage Therapy for Cancer Symptom Care: A systematic review and evidence map.
  • NIH R21 on Dopamine Metabolism in Insomnia Interventions (Acupuncture vs CBT).

Past Projects: Funded by MSKCC, NSFC, HMRF, and others focusing on sleep, blood pressure, pain, and gut microbiome.
