about us

Welcome to iHealth Lab!

iHealth is a young lab dedicated to integrative healthcare research, teaching and educating. We welcome inquires from undergraduate and gradute students for training programs, peer researchers for multidisciplanry collaborations, and people who are interested in integrative health for meaningful discussions.

Our People
Our lab PI is Mingxiao Yang, MD (CMD), PhD, a physician-scientist specializing in integrative healthcare. Dr. Yang is an Assistant Professor at the School of Biological Sciences and NTU Chinese Medicine Clinic in Nanyang Technological University, Singapore. His work spans from patient-centered clinical research, pedagogical teaching, mentorship, and clinical care (soon to come). Before joining NTU, he was an Instructor in Medicine at Harvard Medical School and a faculty member at the Zakim Center for Integrative Therapies and Healthy Living at Dana-Farber Cancer Institute in Boston, MA. He is adept at clinical and translational research in integrative therapies thanks to extensive training in integrative medicine and cancer symptom care from the University of Hong Kong (mentor: Prof. Lixing Lao) and Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center (mentor: Dr. Jun J. Mao). His expertise includes integrative oncology, evidence-based medicine, metabolomics, host-gut microbiome interaction, and technology-assisted healthcare innovation. Our lab aims to promote evidence-based integrative healthcare for all.

lab focus


We are interested in integrative health in general, with specific focuses on:

1) developing integrative medicine approaches, such as acupuncture, herbal medicine, Tai Chi, and exercise, in improving the management of chronic health conditions, particularly addressing the side effects associated with cancer treatment, metabolic syndrome, and cardiovascular diseases;
2) investigating the neurobiological, psychosocial, and behavioral foundations of sensory function, cognition, and memory interconnections and the impact of integrative medicine therapies, utilizing translational omics, neuroimaging, and quantitative sensory testing; and
3) advocate for the accessibility and evidence-based integration of integrative therapies into practical healthcare settings through digitization, artificial intelligence, virtual delivery, and implementation science.

Project title: Acupuncture Regulates RAAS-Gut Microbiota-SCFAs to Reduce Blood Pressure in Spontaneous Hypertensive Rats (PI)

Project title: Self-Administered Preventative Auricular Acupressure for Young Females with Primary Dysmenorrhea: A Randomized, Placebo-Controlled Trial (Co-I)
